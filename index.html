💰 Insurance & Money
Patients should never be surprised by financial expectations. Introduce costs early, reinforce consistently.
We explain clearly. We do not scare patients. We do not guess costs.
Insurance Patient asks "How much will it cost?"
▾
📣 Say This (Verbatim)
"Great question. Because it depends on your plan, we'll verify your benefits and call/text you with what to expect before your visit."
"Most plans have a copay or coinsurance. We'll confirm that."
✅ Do This Next
SpryPT Document the question and that benefits will be verified
Trello Create task for verification and call/text to patient
Do NOT guess costs. Do NOT scare the patient with high estimates.
First Visit First visit financial setup
▾
📣 Opening
"Before we get started I just want to review how payments work here so there are no surprises."
📣 If Patient Has a Deductible
"Your plan has a deductible. Insurance may apply the visit cost to that deductible until it is met."
📣 If Patient Is Surprised by Cost — 4 Steps
Step 1 — Acknowledge"I understand. Insurance plans can definitely be confusing."
Step 2 — Explain"Based on the information we received from your insurance, this visit is being applied toward your deductible."
Step 3 — Reinforce"We collect patient responsibility during visits so accounts stay current."
Step 4 — Move to payment"Today your portion is $[amount]. How would you like to take care of that?"

📅 Cancellation & Attendance
Clear expectations early prevent difficult conversations later.
"All of our visits are one-on-one with a licensed physical therapist. When an appointment is canceled late or missed, we usually can't give that time to another patient who needs care."
Policy When to introduce the cancellation policy (non-negotiable)
▾
📣 1️⃣ When Booking
"Just so you know, we ask for at least 24 hours notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. Since visits are one-on-one, late changes prevent other patients from getting care."
📣 2️⃣ When Scheduling a Plan of Care
"We're reserving these times just for you, so please give us 24 hours notice if something comes up."
📣 3️⃣ At First Check-In
"Just a reminder, we do have a 24-hour cancellation policy."
📣 4️⃣ In Appointment Reminders (Speaking)
"We'll see you tomorrow at [Time]. Please remember to give us at least 24 hours notice if anything changes."
24h+ Notice Patient cancels with 24+ hours notice
▾
📣 Say This (Verbatim)
"Thanks for letting us know ahead of time. Let's get you back on the schedule."
✅ Do This Next
Reschedule immediately — offer two options
SpryPT Document cancellation reason and new appointment
Late Cancel Patient cancels within 24 hours — SAVE script
▾
⚠️ Goal: save the visit first. Apply policy calmly if they still cancel. Never negotiate or waive independently.
📣 Say This (Verbatim) — 4 Steps
Step 1 — Acknowledge"I understand."
Step 2 — State Policy"Since this is within 24 hours, it does fall under our cancellation policy."
Step 3 — Explain Impact"Because your visit is one-on-one, we don't have enough time to give that spot to another patient who needs care."
Step 4 — Attempt to Save"Before we cancel, would later today or tomorrow work?"
✅ Do This Next
If they reschedule: update appointment, document in SpryPT
If still canceling: apply policy calmly. Do not negotiate.
Reschedule next visit immediately
SpryPT Document late cancel and that script was reviewed
Trello Create follow-up task if attendance pattern developing

No Show Patient answers the no-show call
▾
📣 Say This (Verbatim)
"Thanks for letting me know. I'm glad you're okay."
"Just as a reminder, since our visits are one-on-one, we do ask for at least 24 hours notice if you need to cancel or reschedule so we can offer that time to another patient."
"Let's get you back on track. I can add another visit later this week or an extra visit next week to make up for today. Which would work better for you?"

🗂 Systems: SpryPT & Trello
Two systems. Everything lives in one of them. No exceptions.
If it's not written down, it didn't happen. No verbal-only handoffs. No sticky notes. No "I'll remember."
SpryPT SpryPT — Patient facts and history
▾
SpryPT tells the story. Use it for anything that affects scheduling, insurance, money, attendance, or patient communication.
Log in SpryPT when:
  • Cancel or no-show reason
  • Insurance verified / not verified
  • Authorization needed or obtained
  • Scheduling restrictions noted
  • Copay explained to patient
  • Patient flagged as at-risk for attendance
  • Financial setup reviewed at first visit
  • Cancellation script was reviewed with patient

🎯 Practice Drills
Run these out loud every week. Saying the scripts aloud is what makes them automatic under pressure.
Drill 1
New Patient Booking
Practice a complete new patient call from greeting to close — no loose ends.
1
Practice the greeting with clinic name and your name
2
Ask about the patient's situation and reassure them
3
Collect all demographics and insurance information
4
Offer exactly two appointment times and close
5
State the cancellation policy clearly
6
Confirm appointment back and close the call
Drill 2
Late Cancel Save
Practice acknowledging, stating policy, explaining one-on-one impact, and saving the visit.
1
Acknowledge calmly — don't react
2
State the policy matter-of-factly
3
Explain the one-on-one impact
4
Attempt to save — offer later today or tomorrow
5
If still canceling: apply policy calmly, reschedule next visit, document
Drill 3
No-Show Call
Call at 5 minutes. Lead with concern. Remind policy once. Offer recovery.
1
Practice the call script with concern-first tone
2
Practice the voicemail (concern only — no policy)
3
If they answer: glad they're okay → policy once → recovery offer
4
Offer make-up visit: later this week or extra next week
5
Document in SpryPT; create Trello card if pattern developing
Drill 4
Handoff Drill
Practice creating a complete SpryPT note and Trello card so any staff member can finish without questions.
1
Pick a scenario: late cancel, no-show, or insurance follow-up
2
Write the SpryPT note out loud (what happened, what was said, what's next)
3
Write the Trello card title: "Patient Name — Task"
4
Fill all 5 fields: Clinic, Phone, Why, Last Contact, Exact Next Step
5
Ask: could someone else pick this up right now without asking you anything?
